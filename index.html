Project Scope

Literature Survey

Sustainable Agriculture at its Best: Oyster Mushroom Farming Unveiled

Figure 1. Climatic requirements/ Areas suitable for cultivation[1]

Traditional methods of monitoring mushroom growth, disease detection, and quality assurance often rely on subjective human assessments, leading to inefficiencies and inconsistencies these are the main research gaps. This research aims to address these challenges by developing an innovative IoT-based monitoring system that leverages robotic camera imagery, image processing techniques, IoT device and machine learning algorithms. Solution we used in the proposed system focuses on four main sub-objectives by identifying the optimal harvest time based on the size, morphology, and colour of mushrooms, Detecting diseases at an early stage and recommending appropriate treatments, Implementing quality standards assurance software to enhance product value, Integrating multiple environmental sensors with machine learning algorithms for real-time optimization of crop growth. Impact/Success of the proposed system has the potential to revolutionize mushroom cultivation practices by improving efficiency, productivity, and profitability. The system can help farmers to reduce crop losses, enhance product quality, and optimize crop growth. Overall, the proposed IoT-based mushroom cultivation monitoring system offers a promising solution for addressing key challenges in the mushroom cultivation industry. By leveraging advanced technologies such as robotics, image processing, and machine learning, the system can help farmers to make informed decisions and improve their bottom line.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Identification & classification

While the research emphasizes the use of image processing and machine learning for identifying optimal harvest periods and prevalent diseases, there may be a need for further exploration into more diverse and complex mushroom varieties. The study primarily focuses on common diseases found in Sri Lanka, and extending this research to encompass a broader range of mushroom species could enhance its applicability on a global scale.

Severity assessment

The article discusses early-stage detection of diseases, but there is a potential research gap in quantifying and assessing the severity of identified diseases. Developing a robust severity assessment model could provide farmers with a more nuanced understanding of the potential impact of diseases on crop yield and quality. This would enable them to prioritize and tailor remedies based on the severity of the identified issues.

Information sharing

The research touches upon the development of user-friendly mobile interfaces for farmers, but the extent and efficiency of information sharing could be further investigated. Exploring ways to enhance real-time communication between the monitoring system and farmers, perhaps through mobile applications or other digital platforms, could bridge the gap between data analysis and actionable insights. Additionally, considering the socio-economic context of mushroom cultivation in different regions may influence the design and effectiveness of these information-sharing mechanisms.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

The proposed research encounters challenges related to the scalability and adaptability of the IoT-oriented monitoring system for mushroom cultivation. Specifically, there are concerns regarding the system's scalability across diverse mushroom varieties globally, as the research predominantly focuses on common diseases in Sri Lanka. Additionally, the adaptation of the system to various local contexts poses a significant hurdle, considering the distinct agricultural practices, environmental factors, and socio-economic conditions in different regions. These challenges highlight the need to develop a system that can effectively address diseases across a broader spectrum of mushroom species and seamlessly integrate into varied global agricultural landscapes.


Proposed Solution

To mitigate scalability concerns, the research can delve into enhancing machine learning models by training them on a more extensive dataset that includes a diverse range of mushroom species. Advanced algorithms, such as transfer learning, could be employed to improve the system's capability to recognize and respond to diseases universally. Simultaneously, a focus on customization and localization within the IoT monitoring system is vital. This involves collaborating with local agricultural experts to tailor disease detection algorithms to prevalent diseases in specific regions, accommodating variations in climate, and considering cultural practices in mushroom cultivation. Implementing customizable features in the user interface would empower farmers to adapt the system to their specific needs, ensuring a more inclusive and adaptable solution. Additionally, fostering community engagement through surveys, workshops, and continuous feedback sessions with farmers from different regions would provide valuable insights, guiding the refinement of the system to align with the practical realities of mushroom cultivation in diverse contexts.

Research Objectives

Precision Mushroom Harvesting

Developed an IoT based monitoring system (using a robot camera) to identify when to harvest mushroom using image processing and machine learning techniques by considering the shape, size, morphology and the color of mushrooms.

Disease Detection and Recommendations for Mushroom

Developed an IoT-based monitoring system for disease detection and recommendation of mushrooms in Sri Lanka could be to provide farmers with timely and accurate information about the health of their mushrooms. This objective can be achieved by using the IoT-based monitoring system(using a robo camera) to detect diseases at an early stage and recommend appropriate treatment options to farmers.

Environmental Optimization for Crop Growth

Developed an IoT device to integrate multiple environmental sensors, uses machine learning algorithms to analyze sensor data, continuously monitors and manages environmental factors in real-time systems to optimize crop growth.

Revolutionizing Mushroom Quality Assurance

Developed a Quality standards assuring software using image processing and pre-trained models to increase the product value on the local market and international as well.

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The research methodology was enriched through a hands-on exploration at 'Hansi Mushroom,' providing crucial insights into the intricacies of mushroom cultivation. This on-site experience played a pivotal role in shaping the innovative IoT-driven monitoring system. The architecture seamlessly integrated a robotic camera, image processing techniques, and advanced machine learning algorithms, utilizing hardware components such as 'Raspberry Pi Camera V3' and 'Raspberry Pi Camera Model 4.' The objectives spanned disease detection, environmental parameter tracking, harvest time prediction, and quality assurance, with a focus on the American Oyster Mushroom.

For disease detection, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and image processing were harnessed, yielding a dataset of 500 images to identify prevalent diseases and damaging insects. The environmental tracking module incorporated an array of sensors monitored by the Arduino UNO WIFI micro-controller, employing the Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) algorithm for machine learning-based recommendations. Harvest time prediction utilized K-means clustering, Support Vector Machines (SVM), and CNNs, with user-friendly interfaces for farmer input. Quality assurance relied on TensorFlow-powered models, achieving a remarkable 98% enhancement. Results demonstrated high accuracies, affirming the potential of the integrated methodologies in revolutionizing disease detection, environmental surveillance, harvest timing, and product quality assurance in mushroom cultivation.
